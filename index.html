Alistair Inglis

Writer, comedian based in Glasgow.

Get in touch

Hello

Alistair started writing, producing, directing and performing comedy with the Oxford Revue in 2015. He's since honed a distinctive brand of comedy that is simultaneously dumb and thoughtful. As a founding member of Horseplay, he has since toured internationally with them following the success of Witch Hunt at the 2018 Edinburgh Fringe, and was a 2019 Sketch Off semi finalist. He currently lives in Glasgow, focussing on writing.

Work

Performing

In comedy (and life) I am drawn to strong characters, and have the most fun sliding into another pair of shoes for a while. Some favourites shoes at the moment belong to: Tommy Neck, a men's rights activist with a heart of fool's gold, Paulo D'Pesto, everyone's favourite misguided TV chef, and Mary MacRel, a glam Glaswegian SNP councillor.

Writing

In addition to countless sketches, I've written an hour-long comedy show, Witch Hunt and a pilot for its 5-part TV adaptation; Homesick, a warm and absurd family sitcom; Boxed In a short, darkly comic radio play; and Carol: Singer, a short mockumentary following the over-the-hill light entertainer, Carol Singer.

Producing

I've produced a monthly student comedy night, culminating in the annual showcase, the Oxford Revue and Friends at the Oxford Playhouse to over 500 people. In 2018, I produced Funraiser, a mixed-bill fundraiser at the Leicester Square Theatre. At the Edinburgh Fringe I've produced five shows: Oxford Revue Free (2017, 2018), Sons of Butchers (2018), and Witch Hunt (2017, 2018), followed by its international tour in 2019.
